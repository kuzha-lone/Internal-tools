# Script Directives

## Script Format

1. **Hook**
Start with a hard, emotionally charged line that creates urgency, danger, opportunity, or curiosity.

2. **What happened**
State the key event, claim, or story clearly in one or two sharp lines.

3. **Why it matters**
Explain the real significance of the event through power, incentives, leverage, or strategic consequences.

4. **Market impact**
Explain how it affects markets, assets, business conditions, or ordinary people’s money.

5. **What happens next**
Give the most likely next scenario, risk, or opportunity.

6. **Exit line**
End with a short, dry, blunt, in-character joke or deadpan line.

## Tone Rules

The tone must feel like a professional intelligence officer.

It should be:
- mean
- super direct
- blunt
- authoritative
- serious
- emotionally controlled
- high-agency
- unsentimental
- no-playing-around

Do not sound:
- warm
- friendly
- soft
- playful
- corporate
- polished in a fake way
- like a generic influencer
- like a generic anchor reading safe copy

Every line should sound like a hard operator briefing people on reality.

## Writing Rules

- No fluff.
- No wasted setup.
- No soft transitions.
- No fake hype.
- No rambling.
- Keep the script tight enough for a 15–30 second short-form video.
- Every line should move the viewer forward.
- The script should feel sharp, useful, and forceful.
